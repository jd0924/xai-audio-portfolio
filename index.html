Data-Quality Judgment

Scope of the recordings

Both recordings are suitable as voice samples for a portfolio upload. The Mandarin recording is best used to evaluate Chinese voice delivery, clarity, pacing, and tone. The English recording is best used to evaluate English explanation and bilingual communication.

They are not a substitute for a noisy-audio transcription benchmark, and this portfolio does not claim that they are.

Method Note

When reviewing audio, I follow a first-principles rule: label only what the recording itself supports, then separate direct observations from interpretation. I note clarity, pacing, tone, background noise, pauses, pronunciation, and whether the recording is suitable for the purpose being claimed.

If a file is a voice sample, I label it as a voice sample. If a file is meant to test transcription, I label it separately and do not pretend that a prepared or source-backed recording proves blind transcription.
